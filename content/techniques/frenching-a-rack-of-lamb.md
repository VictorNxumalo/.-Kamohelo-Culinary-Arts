steps:
  - "Place rack bone-side up. Identify the flap of meat and fat covering the bones."
  - "Using a sharp boning knife, score along each bone to expose clean rib bones."
  - "Peel away the flap in one piece where possible; trim excess fat leaving a thin cap over the eye of meat."
  - "Scrape between bones with the knife to remove membrane and silverskin for a clean 'handle'."
  - "Trim the fat cap to an even thickness (~5 mm) for uniform cooking."
  - "Tie the rack with butcher's twine if needed to maintain shape during roasting."
tips:
  - "A flexible boning knife gives better control between bones than a chef's knife."
  - "Save trimmed scraps for stocks or staff meals — nothing wasted in a professional kitchen."
  - "Chill the rack briefly if the fat becomes too soft to work cleanly."
---

Frenching transforms a rack of lamb into a refined centrepiece suitable for fine dining service. The technique is as much about presentation as it is about even heat transfer during roasting.
