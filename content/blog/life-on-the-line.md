Before entrepreneurship, before ghost kitchen concepts, before private dining — there is the line.

Working as a line cook taught me that culinary arts is not only creativity. It is repetition under pressure. It is consistency when the dining room is full and the pass is hot. It is respecting the brigade, the timer, and the guest waiting on the other side of the kitchen door.

## The rhythm of service

On a busy service, there is no room for ego. You learn to communicate in short, clear calls. You learn that a perfectly seared protein means nothing if the garnish is late. The line demands **synchronization** — every cook moving with purpose.

## What I carry forward

The discipline from those services now informs everything I build under Kamohelo Culinary Group:

- **Preparation** — mise en place is not optional; it is the foundation of calm service.
- **Consistency** — guests return for reliability as much as for flair.
- **Respect for craft** — technique is earned through hours, not shortcuts.

## Looking ahead

This foundation is why I invest in recipe development, technique documentation, and ventures that honour real kitchen work — not just the highlight reel.
