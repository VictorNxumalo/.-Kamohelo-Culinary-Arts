Pan-searing is deceptively simple. Dry protein. Hot pan. Patience. Yet it is one of the techniques I return to again and again — in fine dining plates, in recipe development, and when teaching fundamentals.

## Heat and moisture

The enemy of a golden crust is surface moisture. Professional kitchens obsess over drying fillets and letting proteins temper slightly before they hit the pan. Home cooks can achieve the same result with paper towel and a heavy skillet.

## The flip rule

Resist the urge to move the fish. Let the pan do the work. When the crust is ready, the protein releases naturally. That moment — the clean lift of a spatula — is one of the small satisfactions of cookery.

## Why it matters for menu development

A reliable sear gives you a foundation. Once you own the technique, you can build sauces, garnishes, and service styles around it without fighting the protein.
