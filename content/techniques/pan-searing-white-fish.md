steps:
  - "Start with dry fillets — moisture is the enemy of browning. Pat thoroughly with paper towel."
  - "Season just before cooking; pre-salting too early can draw moisture to the surface."
  - "Use a heavy pan preheated over medium-high heat with a thin film of high-smoke-point oil."
  - "Place fish skin-side down and resist moving it until the skin releases naturally (3–5 minutes)."
  - "Flip once; add butter and aromatics. Baste the flesh side briefly."
  - "Remove when flesh is opaque and flakes at the thickest point; rest 1 minute before plating."
tips:
  - "Press gently with a fish spatula for the first 30 seconds to prevent curling."
  - "If skinless, sear the presentation side first for colour."
  - "Finish thicker fillets in a 180°C oven after searing both sides."

Pan-searing is a foundational skill for seafood cookery — the goal is maximum crust with a gentle, moist interior. This technique underpins the portfolio pan-seared white fish dish.
